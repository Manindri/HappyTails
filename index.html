Tailored Insights for Happier Tails

In our project, we use advanced machine learning to analyze various aspects of dogs, from predicting their age and breed to identifying skin conditions and emotions. We provide pet owners with valuable insights to ensure the well-being and happiness of their canine companions.

Literature Survey

The "Happy Tails" research project focuses on predicting key aspects of canine well-being, including dog age, breed, emotions, and skin conditions. Existing research has delved into dog age prediction, with methods ranging from dental health and skeletal characteristics to deep learning using facial images. Dog breed classification has employed image recognition and deep convolutional networks. Understanding dog emotions has been explored through behavioral analysis and facial expression recognition. Skin disease detection in dogs has utilized image analysis. This literature survey forms the basis for integrating and extending these findings to create a comprehensive predictive system for dog care in "Happy Tails."

Research Overview and Research Gap

The "Happy Tails" research project endeavors to create an innovative and holistic solution for enhancing canine well-being through predictive analytics. Our goal is to develop a comprehensive system that predicts essential elements of a dog's life, including age, breed, emotions, and skin health, with a focus on integrating these aspects to provide a more profound understanding of a dog's overall condition and needs. Building upon existing research in dog age prediction, breed classification, emotion recognition, and skin disease detection, our approach seeks to bridge the gap in the literature by combining these facets into a unified, user-friendly platform. By leveraging advanced machine learning techniques and image analysis, "Happy Tails" aims to empower pet owners and veterinarians with insights that promote better care and understanding of their four-legged companions. This research overview outlines our commitment to creating a practical and valuable resource for the broader dog-loving community, ensuring happier and healthier "Happy Tails.

While existing research has made significant strides in individual aspects of canine well-being prediction, such as dog age, breed, emotions, and skin disease detection, there is a notable gap in the literature for an integrated and holistic approach that combines these elements. "Happy Tails" seeks to bridge this gap by developing a unified predictive system that addresses the interconnected nature of these factors in dogs' health and happiness, offering a unique and comprehensive solution for pet owners and veterinarians.

Research Problem

In the realm of dog shelters, dog day care centers, and dog care facilities, there is a growing need for a practical and cost-effective solution that empowers professionals and caregivers to identify essential factors related to dogs' age, breed, emotions, and skin health without the necessity of constant consultation with veterinarians. The lack of an accessible and integrated tool tailored for these settings has led to a significant gap in delivering optimal care and understanding of dogs' well-being. This research problem aims to address the challenge of developing a user-friendly, data-driven system, such as the "Happy Tails" project, that enables non-veterinary staff to gain valuable insights into these crucial aspects of canine health. The goal is to create a versatile application that supports dog shelters, day care centers, and care facilities in providing the best care possible to their furry residents, promoting their happiness and overall welfare.

Research Objectives

This research project is dedicated to developing a Machine Learning-driven system that advances the quality of care provided by dog care centers, with the ultimate goal of ensuring the well-being of every canine resident. The research will focus on achieving the following objectives: first, creating a comprehensive dog health profiling system that encompasses factors like age, breed, emotions, and skin health. Second, the system will be designed to collect real-time data through visual, auditory, and behavioral cues, providing continuous, up-to-date insights. The third objective is to enhance diagnostic accuracy by employing advanced machine learning algorithms and models in age prediction, breed classification, emotion recognition, and skin disease detection. Fourth, a user-friendly interface will be crafted for easy accessibility and interpretation by dog care professionals. The system will be personalized for each individual dog, optimizing resource allocation and support. Its impact on dog well-being will be thoroughly evaluated. Ethical and privacy considerations will be central to its design. Lastly, mechanisms for continuous improvement will be established to ensure the system adapts to evolving canine needs. These objectives collectively drive our mission to revolutionize the care dogs receive in dog care centers, promoting their health, happiness, and overall quality of life.

Technologies

Flask

Flask is a lightweight and versatile Python web framework known for its simplicity and ease of use. It is widely used for building web applications, APIs, and microservices. Flask's minimalist design and extensive ecosystem of extensions make it a popular choice for web developers seeking flexibility and rapid development.

Tensorflow

TensorFlow is a free and open-source software library for dataflow and differentiable programming across a range of tasks. It is a symbolic math library, and is also used for machine learning applications such as neural networks.

React

React, a JavaScript library for building user interfaces, played a pivotal role in our project's frontend development. We utilized React to create a dynamic and responsive user interface, enabling real-time interactions and seamless updates. Its component-based architecture facilitated the modular design of our application, allowing for easy integration of features and ensuring a smooth user experience

Keras

Keras is an open-source library that provides a Python interface for artificial neural networks. Keras acts as an interface for the TensorFlow library. Up until version 2.3 Keras supported multiple backends, including TensorFlow, Microsoft Cognitive Toolkit, R, Theano, and PlaidML.
